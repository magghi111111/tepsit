The Web

The website gives an overview of the key people who have shaped the web from its beginning, starting with Tim Berners-Lee's groundbreaking invention of the World Wide Web, which changed the way we share information globally. The development of web browsers saw big improvements from Berners-Lee's first browser to Marc Andreessen's Mosaic, which made it popular to use images on web pages, setting up the internet to grow dynamically.

Justin Hall and Ev Williams were super important in the creation of personal web logs, or blogs, making it easier and more fun to create online content. Ward Cunningham's invention of the first wiki helped a lot with sharing information together, leading to Wikipedia being created by Jimmy Wales. Wikipedia's open-editing model made it one of the biggest and most complete sources of information online.

Larry Page and Sergey Brin, the founders of Google, changed how we search the web with their PageRank algorithm, making it easier and more reliable to find information. Other pioneers like Jerry Yang and David Filo of Yahoo! also had a big impact on how people use and interact with the web, shaping the digital world we know today.
